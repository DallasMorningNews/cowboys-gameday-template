Cowboys Gameday

San Francisco 49ers at Cowboys

3:25 Sunday TV: Ch. 4

Radio: KRLD-FM (105.3), KMVK-FM (107.5 in Spanish)

Line: 49ers by 5

By Rainer Sabin | Staff Writer

    A win would mean ...

    The Cowboys win their third consecutive season opener and improve their all-time record against San Francisco in regular-season and playoff games to 17-16-1.

    A loss would mean ...

    No surprises. The Cowboys aren’t favored to win this game. But a defeat would prolong the negativity surrounding a franchise that went 0-4 in the preseason.

    Four downs Key to the game

    The Cowboys are 11-0 when DeMarco Murray carries the ball at least 20 times in a game. (Vernon Bryant/Staff Photographer)

    • 1

      Protect Romo

      Shielding Tony Romo will be the top priority for the Cowboys all season. They have no chance without their veteran quarterback. Romo is 34 and barely eight months removed from major back surgery. He completed 60.9 percent of his preseason pass attempts for 167 yards — hardly enough quality work to dispense with doubts about his durability. The 49ers’ pass rush is weakened because of the suspension of Aldon Smith. But this defense still can generate sustained pressure that could pose problems for Romo.

    • 2

      Limit the 49ers rushing offense

      The Cowboys’ run defense during the preseason was unsightly. Opponents averaged 4.3 yards per carry while Dallas’ frontline players struggled to penetrate or shed blocks. This can’t happen against the 49ers. The Cowboys will have to be disciplined with their run fits and understand their gap responsibilities as they try to limit San Francisco quarterback Colin Kaepernick, a speedy threat who rushed for a career-high 524 yards last season. Kaepernick and perennial Pro Bowl running back Frank Gore must be contained if the Cowboys are to have a chance.

    • 3

      Force turnovers

      The Cowboys were at their best last season when they were able to take the ball away from their opponents. In the first half of the 2013 campaign, the Cowboys created nine more turnovers than they committed. When they won during that eight-game stretch, their average margin was 14.5 points. When they lost, the average margin was only 3.5 points. The numbers indicate the Cowboys can hang with anybody if they can generate takeaways. But here’s the problem: No team has committed fewer turnovers than the 44 the 49ers have coughed up since 2011.

    • 4

      Establish running game

      The Cowboys said throughout the offseason that they would show a stronger allegiance to the running game than in recent seasons. It’s important that they follow through on that commitment. The Cowboys are 11-0 when DeMarco Murray carries the ball at least 20 times in a game. A strong performance by Murray could not only help Dallas defeat San Francisco, but it could set the tone for the rest of the season as the Cowboys try to prove they are a physical bunch with their revamped offensive line.

    How they match up

    When the Cowboys run the ball

    <

    First-year Cowboys play-caller Scott Linehan has vowed to make a commitment to the running game. Dallas ran the ball only 336 times in 2013, second fewest behind Atlanta. In 2013, San Francisco had the fourth-best run defense. But the 49ers’ front seven won’t include Glenn Dorsey, NaVorro Bowman or Aldon Smith. But defensive tackle Ray McDonald will play. Edge: Cowboys

    When the Cowboys pass the ball

    Tony Romo hasn’t played a full game since undergoing surgery to repair a herniated disk Dec. 27. But he has a bevy of offensive weapons, foremost among them Pro Bowlers Dez Bryant and Jason Witten. The 49ers curtailed big plays last season, conceding only 48 of 20 yards or more. Edge: Cowboys

    When the 49ers run the ball

    For the first time in a while, the 49ers’ offensive line has some question marks. Whether that will affect Frank Gore’s productivity is uncertain. Gore ran for 1,128 yards last season. He forms a fearsome duo with mobile QB Colin Kaepernick, who could present problems for Dallas’ weak defensive front. Edge: 49ers

    When the 49ers pass the ball

    Last season, quarterbacks threw for 400 or more yards against Dallas, an NFL record. This year, the pass defense looks even worse. Orlando Scandrick, the team’s top cornerback in 2013, is suspended for the first four games. His replacement, Morris Claiborne, didn’t play in the preseason. Edge: 49ers

    Special teams

    The Cowboys excelled in special teams last season. And while there has been considerable turnover within each of the units, the key contributors remain. They include Dan Bailey, who has converted 90.8 percent of his field goal attempts. San Francisco’s Phil Dawson struggled in the preseason, making only four of six tries. Edge: Cowboys

    Intangibles

    A tumultuous 0-4 preseason has ended for the Cowboys. But it seems certain that it will continue to haunt them. The Cowboys, who are 22-18 at AT&T Stadium, don’t have the distinct home-field advantage necessary overcome the bad karma that seems to have afflicted the franchise. Edge: 49ers

    Spotlight

    Questions linger about how well Tony Romo will rebound from his offseason back surgery. (Vernon Bryant/Staff Photographer)

    Romo carries over doubts from offseason

    Throughout the offseason, the attention of Cowboys followers has been focused on the condition of Tony Romo. He underwent back surgery Dec. 27 to repair a herniated disk. More than eight months later, he’s fit to play.

    But questions persist about the 34-year-old quarterback, who enjoyed one of his most productive seasons in 2013. Though he’s not far removed from producing 3,828 passing yards, 31 touchdown passes and a 96.7 efficiency rating, it’s uncertain whether Romo can continue to perform at that level.

    “I think he’s going to be great,” tight end Jason Witten said. “It’s been a good offseason for him. I think he’s worked really hard at doing all the little things that allow him to be successful and get back, and I thought throughout training camp he got better and better and better ... .”

    Romo, with a certain measure of nonchalance, says he’s prepared to do just that.

    “You’ve just got to go play,” he said.

    It’s his first opportunity to show he is the Tony Romo of old.

    Fantasy Corner

    Two to start

    Dez Bryant, WR, Cowboys

    Scott Linehan’s track record suggests that Bryant will get a lot of opportunities this season. He should have his way against 49ers cornerbacks Tramaine Brock and Chris Culliver, who have started a combined 13 games.

    Frank Gore, RB, 49ers

    The Cowboys’ run defense looked horrible in the preseason. Gore has been selected to the Pro Bowl the last three seasons. He should feast on Dallas.

    Two to sit

    Gavin Escobar, TE, Cowboys

    Escobar missed the last two preseason games with a right shoulder injury, and it remains to be seen whether he will be a featured part of the offense this season.

    Phil Dawson, K, 49ers

    The Lake Highlands product missed two field goal tries in the preseason, raising concerns about his accuracy. The normally reliable kicker seems capable of getting back on track, but it may make sense to wait a week to see if in fact he does.
